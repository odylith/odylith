# Surface Truth

Odylith v0.1.9 makes the product surfaces feel trustworthy again: they open the
right thing, say less nonsense, and hold together under proof.

- Radar relation chips, cross-surface deep links, and filter search now stay on
  the governed record you asked for instead of wandering into stale
  selections.
- Maintainer-only autofix and traceability noise no longer leaks into default
  operator warnings, so Radar and Compass tell the same calmer story.
- The shell keeps first-run welcome and upgrade story separate, uses the
  canonical hosted installer path, and treats the authored release note as the
  upgrade source of truth.
- Registry and Atlas now model the memory substrate as a first-class part of
  the product, while ambient Odylith guidance stays benchmark-safe and quiet by
  default.
