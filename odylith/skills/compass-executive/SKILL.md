# compass-executive

Use when you need Compass-driven executive or operator summaries over recent work, current execution, and next actions.

## Canonical Commands

```bash
odylith compass log --repo-root . --kind decision --summary "<decision>"
odylith compass log --repo-root . --kind implementation --summary "<implementation update>"
odylith compass update --repo-root . --statement "<current execution state>"
odylith compass refresh --repo-root . --wait
odylith sync --repo-root . --force
```

## Rules

- Keep Compass narrative concise, execution-meaningful, grounded in local repo truth, and in plainspoken grounded maintainer narration.
- Use ordinary words a maintainer can understand on first read. Stay open, natural, clear, and lightly soulful without sounding polished or performed.
- Stock framing is forbidden. Do not use repeated house phrases, queue-label restatement, repeated window leads, generic priority or attention wrappers, sloganized self-host status, rhetorical benchmark challenges, or canned `next/current/why/timing` scaffolding.
- Stagey metaphor is forbidden too. Do not write `pressure point`, `center of gravity`, `muddy`, `slippery`, `top lane`, `window coverage spans`, or other dashboard-wise phrases that sound vivid but explain nothing.
- Bullet labels like `Executive/Product` and `Operator/Technical` are forbidden too. Compass briefs are unlabeled narrative bullets now; do not reintroduce a voice split in cached prose, copied summaries, or compatibility rewrites.
- Every bullet must stay tethered to the cited fact language. If it still reads clean after the facts are stripped away, it is too generic for Compass.
- Apply that ban to provider output, warmed cache reuse, deterministic fallback, and any manual Compass-facing summary you write.
- If a Compass summary sounds templated, rhythmic, or dashboard-polished, treat that as a product regression in governed truth or narrator policy, not as a copy-polish pass.
- Keep the normal refresh path cheap. Timeline material should be reused from deterministic Compass state, and simple brief enrichment should prefer `gpt-5.3-codex-spark` at low reasoning rather than a heavyweight provider session.
- Cheap also means selective. Reuse the last validated live brief when the
  narrative-relevant window signature is unchanged, and rebuild only the
  scopes or windows that actually moved.
- Use the shared Scope Signal Ladder for scoped budget decisions. Quiet scopes
  do not get fresh provider narration just because they are selected.
- Fresh scoped provider narration is reserved for `R4-R5` scopes. Lower rungs
  stay cache or deterministic, and `R0-R1` deep links remain quiet instead of
  borrowing global activity.
- Prefer Compass for recent activity and current execution posture, not as a replacement for direct bug/plan/workstream inspection.
